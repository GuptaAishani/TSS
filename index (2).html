Purpose

Our Mission

Texas Space Society is UT Austin's premier student organization dedicated to advancing the frontiers of space exploration. We bring together the brightest engineers, scientists, and dreamers to tackle real space challenges — from designing lunar habitats to building flight hardware that actually launches.

Our members work alongside industry leaders at NASA, SpaceX, Blue Origin, Firefly Aerospace, and beyond — because we believe hands-on experience is the rocket fuel of careers.

We don't just study space. We build for it.

🚀
Technical Excellence
Real engineering on real hardware. Every team produces deliverables that matter — from CFD-analyzed airframes to orbital habitat proposals.
🌕
Long-Term Vision
Permanent human presence beyond Earth. We design for the civilizations that will follow us into the cosmos.
🤝
Industry Connection
Direct pathways to internships and full-time roles at top aerospace companies through mentorship and project-based learning.
⚡
Launch Culture
Move fast, build things, learn from failure, and iterate — the same ethos that drives the new space industry.
Divisions

Technical Teams

01 / STRUCTURES
Space Systems Integration
Design modular orbital and lunar habitat architectures. Structural hull analysis, composite layering, and rotational gravity systems for permanent settlements.
CAD · FEA · Composites
02 / PROPULSION
Liquid Rocket Engine Team
Design, manufacture, and hot-fire liquid bipropellant rocket engines. From injector design to combustion chamber analysis — real hardware, real burns.
CFD · Thermal · Hotfire
03 / AVIONICS
Flight Systems & GNC
Embedded flight computers, sensor fusion, and guidance algorithms. Build the brains that navigate our vehicles from launch to landing.
RTOS · Kalman · PCB
04 / MISSION OPS
Mission Design & Analysis
Orbital mechanics, trajectory optimization, and mission planning. Develop concepts that compete in NASA and AIAA challenge programs.
GMAT · STK · Python
05 / ROBOTICS
Lunar Rover & Robotics
Autonomous surface exploration vehicles. Mechanical design, terrain navigation, and remote operations for lunar and planetary environments.
ROS · SLAM · ML
06 / PAYLOAD
Science & Payload Systems
Experiment design, payload integration, and scientific instrumentation for high-altitude balloon and small satellite missions.
CubeSat · HAB · Sensors
